Hi, I am Xinyu Shi, a fifth-year Ph.D. candidate at University of Waterloo in Canada. I work on Human-Computer Interaction, Human-Centered AI, and Computational Creativity. I am lucky to be advised by Prof. Jian Zhao in WVisdom Research Group, affiliated with the WaterlooHCI Lab. During my PhD, I have interned at Adobe Research (twice) and Microsoft Research Asia. My work has been recognized with a Best Paper Award and an Honorable Mention Award at CHI.

My research defines new interaction paradigms for AI-powered creativity support tools that make the implicit structures behind the creative process explicit to augment human’s creative thinking and align machine-generated results with human intention. In specific, I design meaningful and useful interactive visual abstractions to represent and manipulate visual media, which serve as the interaction mediator between human creators and machine intelligence to enable powerful ways of thinking, communication, and design.

Outside of research, I am keen on exploring new possibilities in my life. I love skiing, playing badminton, rock climbing (only belay, no bouldering), and travelling. I am the mom of two cats (a boy and a girl), they are super sweet!
